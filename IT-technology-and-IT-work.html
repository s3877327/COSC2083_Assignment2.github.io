Virtual reality/Augmented reality:

Virtual reality (VR) is the result of using computer science to create an environment that can be either similar or different from the world that we are currently living in. With VR, one can create a totally immersive environment by using an application programming interface that supports VR devices, such as WebVR. To make VR fully immersive, all senses must be under the control of the system, and to feed data from the virtual world to the user, who is still staying in the realm of the real, special VR gears are needed. The most basic, which is the most iconic when talking about virtual technology is the head-mounted display. It creates the feeling of virtual stimulation by generate two images and display those images from two different perspectives while also enhance that experience by incorporating features such as head-tracking. Along with the famous headset, there are also wire gloves, a 3D mouse, motion controller, and optical sensors to further enhance the experience. There are also VR cameras that can be used to create VR 360 interactive photography, but with low resolutions or in compressed format.

Augmented reality is the result of using computer systems to augment real-world scenery include extra information, sound, or filter, and allow real-time interaction between the user and the system, while the user still maintains a presence in the real world, with most scenes unaffected and not under control by the system. AR works by register objects from the real world in the scene, then insert the enhancement graphic, audio, or other senses and then track them with the 3D objects and change them in real-time, depending on the user’s action. Augmented reality systems need three main components to function properly: a display, a tracking system, and a computing device. Luckily, all three of them are already integrated into the average smartphone that a person normally owns, with the phone’s cameras act as both the display and serve the purpose of tracking while the phone’s computational power is enough to fill the role of being the AR system’s computation device.

Both AR and VR have the enormous capability in a wide range of fields from military to entertainment, training, medical, ...

- Applications of VR:

In the entertainment industry, cinemas have been using this technology for a long time, notably with the service of 3D cinema. Videogames with VR compatibility has seen an explosion of number and interest since the 2010s, with a new generation of commercial headsets released by HTC, Oculus, and Sony that makes VR becomes cheaper and more accessible to the mass. The idea of events hosted in the virtual realm has been in consideration for a long time. Thanks to the outbreak, this idea has become a way for events to be hosted without causing any health and safety hazards

VR technology also has the potential for usage in the medical industry. Medical schools can train their students using simulated VR programs for high-risk operations such as surgeries without risking the lives of patients in the hand of inexperienced students.

Due to its nature of creating an immersive environment, virtual reality can have a big impact on education and training. Courses that used to be very expensive like engineering or very dangerous such as parachuting, or combat drill can now become cheaper thanks to the fact that students can interact with 3D objects and it will react accordingly that is similar to field practice.

The videogame industry also implements AR, creating games that let the user experience digital gameplay play layered with real-world elements. An example of this is Niantic’s release called Pokémon Go, which became one of the biggest hits in the world in 2016.

A developer’s workday usually starts by checking mails for updates and communication between members and between departments. Their work is mainly about software and programs. And as such, a large part of their daily work revolves around coding and debugging. They can work as freelancers hired by clients or work in corporations in projects both large and small. Besides coding, he also has to work effectively in a group. For that, He has to go to meetings for discussions about the situation and the project’s path. They also frequently check their repositories on services such as GitHub and code review, so that codes with errors don’t get to the production phase. Besides interaction with their peers or with their clients, they also interact with the corporation’s department in the form of surveys. These surveys either be daily or happens once a year. In the modern workplace, in the West at least can have a very flexible workplace, with they can spend their time working either in the office or at home. However, due to the outbreak and the subsequent lockdown, working in the office has become a safety hazard and such, most of their work is done at home. The most challenging part of becoming a developer is to effectively cooperate with people as a group to produce results that can satisfy the requirements without being heavily affected by internal friction and delays.
